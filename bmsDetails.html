Smart Battery Management System (BMS)

The Smart Battery Management System (BMS) is a real-time battery monitoring and protection system designed for Li-ion and LiFePO4 battery packs used in electric vehicles, e-bikes, and energy storage systems. It continuously monitors critical battery parameters and ensures safe operation at all times.

The system uses an INA219 current sensor along with a voltage divider and temperature sensor to measure battery voltage, current, temperature, State of Charge (SOC) and State of Health (SOH). All live data is displayed on a 0.96" OLED screen and transmitted over CAN bus to other system nodes.

Working Principle

  • INA219 sensor measures current and bus voltage continuously
  • Analog temperature sensor reads battery pack temperature
  • MCU calculates SOC and SOH based on readings
  • OLED displays all parameters in real-time
  • CAN transceiver sends data to other BMS or vehicle ECU nodes
  • Protection triggers cut-off relay on fault conditions

Main Components Used

  • Arduino / STM32 Microcontroller
  • INA219 Current and Voltage Sensor
  • NTC Thermistor (Temperature Sensor)
  • 0.96" OLED Display (SSD1306)
  • CAN Bus Transceiver (MCP2551)
  • Li-ion / LiFePO4 Battery Pack
  • Protection Circuit (MOSFETs / Relay)
  • Power Supply and connecting wires

Parameters Monitored

  • Voltage Range: 0 – 60 V
  • Current Range: 0 – 50 A
  • Temperature Range: -20°C to +80°C
  • State of Charge (SOC) in %
  • State of Health (SOH) in %
  • Communication: CAN Protocol

Protection Features

  • Overvoltage protection
  • Undervoltage protection
  • Overcurrent protection
  • Overtemperature protection
  • Short circuit protection

Applications

  • Electric Vehicles (EV) battery packs
  • E-bikes and E-scooters
  • Energy Storage Systems (ESS)
  • Solar power battery banks
