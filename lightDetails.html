Traffic Light Controller

The Traffic Light Controller is a microcontroller-based system that simulates and controls the sequencing of traffic signals at a road intersection. It uses the AT89C51 microcontroller to implement timed red, yellow, and green light sequences that manage vehicle flow efficiently and improve road safety.

The system was designed using embedded C programming and simulated accurately in Proteus software before hardware implementation. Timer concepts are used for precise signal timing — RED for 30 seconds, YELLOW for 5 seconds, and GREEN for 30 seconds — matching real-world traffic light behavior.

Working Principle

  • Microcontroller runs an infinite loop of signal sequencing
  • RED light ON for 30 seconds — vehicles stop
  • YELLOW light ON for 5 seconds — vehicles prepare
  • GREEN light ON for 30 seconds — vehicles move
  • YELLOW again for 5 seconds before switching back to RED
  • ULN2003 driver amplifies microcontroller output to drive LEDs

Main Components Used

  • AT89C51 Microcontroller (8051 family)
  • Crystal Oscillator — 11.0592 MHz
  • ULN2003 Driver IC
  • Red, Yellow, Green LEDs
  • Resistors and capacitors
  • Power Supply — 5V DC
  • Proteus simulation software

Signal Timing Sequence

  • RED → 30 seconds (Stop)
  • YELLOW → 5 seconds (Get Ready)
  • GREEN → 30 seconds (Go)
  • YELLOW → 5 seconds (Slow Down)
  • Cycle repeats continuously

Features

  • Accurate timed sequencing using timer interrupts
  • Microcontroller-based smart and reliable control
  • Low power consumption design
  • Simulated in Proteus for verification before hardware build
  • Scalable to multi-lane intersections

Applications

  • Road intersection traffic management
  • Pedestrian crossing control systems
  • Smart city traffic automation
  • Embedded systems education and demonstration

Future Improvements

  • Implement emergency vehicle override using IR/RF
